Morning Trails

March 3, 2024

The morning starts with mist curling over the ridgeline. Bears aren’t awake yet, but the forest already hums—pines exhaling, creek water stitching its way downhill. I follow the switchbacks slowly, shoes crunching over frost and last season’s leaves.

Halfway up, I unpack a small thermos of tea and a notebook. Thirty minutes of silence becomes a page of quick sketches: the shape of a distant peak, the sharp breath in my chest, the shadows sliding down the valley. Slowness doesn’t fight progress; it just measures it differently.

By the time the sun clears the trees, the trail glows copper. I close the notebook, tuck it in my pack, and head home with fresh ideas for future posts. This site is a record of those tiny moments—a reminder that consistent walks outside keep the writing honest.
